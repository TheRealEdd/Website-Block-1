This is a section about photography.

I've been taking pictures ever since I left secondary school. It started out as a hobby around my graphic design interests, and later developed into a more serious time investment. I have selected a few images here for your enjoyment. Feel free to click on any of them for more information!

Llandeio

The rolling hills as viewed from my grandparents' house in Wales.

Carreg Cennen

This castle ruin has been a part of my childhood for as long as I remember. It is an open site, where you can climb on anything you see.

Abstract Lightfall

As the morning sun shines through the window, the different light and shadow creates an interesting bunch of shapes on the ceiling.

Dunkerque Docks

As my ferry sets out to sea, I take one last look behind me. All I see is a plume of smoke from a nearby factory. A solemn sight in the morning light.

A Macroscopic View

A neat little trick for my fellow photographers out there, turn your lens around, and you can get closer than you ever thought possible. This flower is around a centimetre in diameter.

Nature, Framed.

Sometimes you look up, and realise the interesting contrast between the shaded leaves close, and the far needles basking in the sunlight. This was one of those times.

Narnia?

"As normal as a door or window may seem, you will never know what is on the other side until you look through it" - Schrodinger. Just kidding, but I'm sure he believed it though.

The Severn Bridge

Two tall towers, together bearing load. Humans, small and frail, crossing their sturdy road.

Street Lights

At times, even the most mundane can suddenly seem appealing in the eyes of a bored traveller. I was that bored traveller on the day I took this image.

This is a section about music.

Music has been a big part of my life growing up. I cannot remember when I first started singing and playing instruments, but I know that it was very early. These days I play when I feel like it, and own various instruments and bits of recording equipment that I use to sometimes save what I create. These are some of those fragments of my musical life.

This part will be about my digital art.

As you might have noticed while looking through my music and photography work, I can get sucked into my hobbies with great ease. Digital design is another one of those hobbies that started off as a little bit of fun, and later evolved into a great place to express my more visual art. This can include my photography too at times, but is mostly playing with shapes, colour, and light. Feel free to have a gander.

Icebears Esports

A commissioned logo I made for a friend's esports team.

Moonlit Station

This is a piece I did many years ago. It was created using shapes only.

Self Portrait

This style is very close to me, I used to convert photographs to graphics this way. This image is my steam image as well.

Angry Seagulls

Being born and raised in the sea town of Brighton, UK, seagulls have a nostalgia linked to them for me that I've never quite understood...

Luke's Logo

This image was created when a good friend of mine was looking for a new profile picture for steam. I tried using the shapes of his letters to create an interesting pattern.

Pathfinder, Apex Legends

If you ever find yourself playing Apex Legends, look above you. He prefers to take his prey from the skies.

Look into my iris...

Nowhere on earth do we have a power source strong enough for Pathfinder's energy. I wonder where he came from...

A Space Walk

Sometimes, astronauts too like to just have a stroll through the fields. This photomash was a huge effort, but I'm very happy with how it turned out.

Stormtrooper's Little Helper

This part is for me.

I hope you've enjoyed looking at my work, varying from photography to music and back round to graphic design. Perhaps you would like to get to know more about who I am, and what brought me closer to my hobbies. If that's the kind of section you were looking for then you've found the right place.

A musician by nature, an artist by heart, I've been dabbling in various arts and crafts for as long as I remember.

Working my way through music, then graphic design, and finally photography, I find myself at the crossroads of artistic expression. I have no trouble at all finding a way to portray my views through any of these media and enjoy it thoroughly.
